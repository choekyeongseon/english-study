# 표현 & 어휘 (Expressions & Vocabulary)

자주 쓰이는 표현과 어휘 정리

---

## 인사 표현

| 질문형 | 특징 |
| --- | --- |
| How are you / How are you doing / How is it going | 답변: fine, great, not bad, excellent |
| What's up / What's going on / What's happening | 답변: not so much, how about you? |

---

## 잘 — 다양한 표현

| 한국어 의미 | 영어 | 뉘앙스 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 잘 됐어 (순조롭게 진행됐다) | go well | 일이 잘 진행됐다는 느낌 |
| 잘 풀렸어 (문제가 해결됐다) | work out well | 문제가 해결됐다는 느낌 |
| (기능이) 잘 돼 | work well | 작동이 잘 된다 |
| 잘 (쉽게) 발생한다 | ~ easily | 쉽게 일어난다 |
| 잘 모르겠어 | I'm not sure | 불확실성 표현 |
| ~을 잘 하다 | be good at ~ | I'm really good at math. |
| ~을 잘 다루다 | be good with ~ | He's good with children. |
| ~에 대해 잘 알다 | know a lot about ~ | He knows a lot about politics. |
| ~을 잘 알다 (충분한 인지) | know well | He knows very well how important this was. |

**예시:**

* _How did it go?_ / _Did it go well?_
* _It worked out very well._ (아주 잘 풀렸어)
* _Everything is working well._ (시스템이 다 잘 되고 있어)
* _I forget things so easily these days._ (나 요즘 너무 잘 까먹어)

## 실수였어

* **It was an honest mistake.** — 정말 모르고 한 실수였어
* **I really didn't know.** — 정말 몰랐어 (강조)
* **I didn't really know.** — 잘 몰랐어 (약한 강조)
* **I didn't mean to V / be + 형용사** — ~을 의도한 게 아니었다

    * _I didn't mean to be rude._
    * _I didn't mean to insult her._

## 상관있다/없다

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| matter | 중요하다, 상관있다 | It doesn't matter to me what you do. |
| What does that matter? | 그게 무슨 상관이야? | — |
| have something to do with | ~와 관련이 있다 | What does that have to do with this? |
| have nothing to do with | ~와 아무 상관 없다 | This has nothing to do with you. |
| have a lot to do with | ~와 많은 관련이 있다 | It has a lot to do with the economy. |
| concern | 관련되다 (격식) | This doesn't concern you. |
| be related to | 연관되다 | The two incidents were related to each other. |

## definitely / obviously / noticeably

| 단어 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| definitely (= clearly, certainly) | 확실히 | This one is definitely better. |
| obvious / obviously | 딱 봐도 명백한, 누구나 알 수 있는 | Yeah, I think it's pretty obvious. |
| noticeable / noticeably | 눈에 띄게, 티 나게 | The material is noticeably better. |

## 묘사 지각동사 (Perception Verbs)

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| look + 형용사 | ~해보이다 | It looked really dirty. |
| look like + 명사/문장 | ~처럼 보이다 | It looked like it hadn't been washed in months. |
| it was pretty clear that | 보나마나 ~이었다 | It was pretty clear that it didn't have an owner. |
| almost look as if | 마치 ~처럼 보였다 | It almost looked as if it had nothing left but skin and bones. |

> **관계절 생략**: 묘사 문장에서 흔히 생략 가능
> _I saw a man who was smoking → I saw a man smoking_

## 잘 모르겠다 — 3가지 표현

| 표현 | 뉘앙스 |
| --- | --- |
| I'm not sure. | 확실하지 않아 (일반적) |
| All I know is that ~ | 내가 아는 건 ~뿐이야 |
| I don't know much about that either. | 그것도 잘은 모르겠어 |

## 내가 알기로는 / 듣기로는 / 보기에는

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 내가 알기로는 | As far as I know / As far as I understand |
| 내가 듣기로는 | From what I hear / From what I've heard |
| 개인적으로 전달받은 얘기 | I was told that ~ |
| 내가 보기에는 (내 판단) | The way I see it / In my view |

**예시:**

* _As far as I know, the movie is releasing this week._
* _From what I hear, it's pretty good._
* _The way I see it, timing was the problem._

**+참고:**

* **according to** — ~에 따르면 (공식 출처)
* **based on** — ~에 근거하여 (격식)

## 그래서 — 두 가지 용법

| 용법 | 구조 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 반응 (그래서 ~했다) | so | I hurt my hand, so I went to the hospital. |
| 이유 설명 (그래서 못 한 거야) | and that is why | My phone battery ran out, and that is why I couldn't call you. |

> `so`는 행동을 취한 것, `that is why`는 이유를 설명하는 것

## 누구에게 무엇을 주다

| 구조 | 강조 포인트 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| give something + to someone | 누구에게 주는지 부각 | I gave some money to my parents. |
| give someone + something | 무엇을 주는지 부각 | I gave my parents some money. |

**대명사 사용 시:**

* _(O) I gave it to my parents._ / _(X) I gave my parents it._
* _(O) I gave them some money._ / _(X) I gave some money to them._

## 무조건 / 어떻게든 / 무슨 수를 써서라도

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 무조건 (이번엔) | seriously have to + no matter what |
| 어떻게든 / 어떤 식으로든 | one way or another |
| 무슨 수를 써서라도 | whatever it takes |
| 어쨌든 간에 (2가지 조건 후) | either way |

**예시:**

* _I seriously have to pass the exam this time, no matter what._
* _One way or another, we have to finish this by the end of the month._
* _Smile, apologize, lie — whatever it takes._

## 딱히 / 의외로 / 아무렇지도 않게 / 점차

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 딱히 (특별히) | not particularly |
| 단지 ~해서 그래 | It's just that ~ |
| 의외로 | surprisingly |
| 아무렇지도 않게 (당연하다는 듯이) | like it was nothing |
| 어쩔 수 없이 | can't help + ing |
| 점점, 점차 | it's going to get better / getting + 형용사 |

**예시:**

* _There wasn't particularly a problem, but it's just that people asked me very private questions like it was nothing._
* _I wanted to fit in, so I couldn't help answering their questions._

## 고민이다 (할지 말지)

**구조:** I'm not sure / I'm trying to decide + if/whether + (should) V

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 걔한테 이것에 대해 얘기하는 게 좋을지 고민이야 | I'm not sure if I should tell him about it. |
| 지금 얘기할지 아니면 기다릴지 고민이야 | I'm not sure if I should tell him now, or wait. |
| 어느 것을 사는 게 좋을지 고민이야 | I'm trying to decide which one I should buy. |
| 걔한테 뭐라고 얘기해줄지 모르겠어 | I'm not sure what I should tell him. |

## 하는 것이 더 좋다 / 낫다

| 한국어 | 영어 | 뉘앙스 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ~하는 것이 낫다 | it's better to ~ | 현재/사실 |
| ~하는 것이 나을 거야 | it would be better to ~ | 미래 가정 |
| ~하는 것이 나을 수도 있어 | it might be better to ~ | 가능성 |

## 시간이 걸리다 / 돈이 들다

| 표현 | 예문 |
| --- | --- |
| It takes ~ (시간이 걸리다) | It takes 5 minutes. / It doesn't take very long. |
| It took a long time. | 시간이 많이 걸렸어. |
| It's going to take a lot of time. | 시간이 많이 걸릴 거야. |
| It costs ~ (돈이 들다) | It cost a lot. / It didn't cost very much. |
| It cost more than I thought. | 생각보다 돈이 많이 들었어. |

**질문형:**

* _Does it take long?_ / _How long does it take?_
* _Did it cost a lot?_ / _How much did it cost?_

## pay / cost / afford / charge

| 단어 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| pay | 돈을 내다 | I paid 10 dollars. / Did you pay? |
| pay for | 무엇에 돈을 쓰다 | I paid a lot for this. |
| cost | 돈이 얼마 들다 | It cost almost 1 million won. |
| afford | 살 여건/형편이 되다 | I can't afford this. |
| charge | 청구하다 | The library charged me 5 dollars for returning it late. |

## 기대하다

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| look forward to + ing | 기대하다 (들뜬 마음) | I'm really looking forward to the movie. |
| I was looking forward to ~ | 기대했지만 못 해서 아쉬움 | I was really looking forward to that. |
| I can't wait to V | 너무 기대된다 (빨리 하고 싶다) | I can't wait to graduate and start making money. |
| expect + 사람 + to V | 예상하다 (이런 일이 있을 거다) | You must not expect your parents to do everything for you. |

## 영향을 받다 / 주다

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| be influenced by | 간접적 영향을 받다 | She was influenced by a lot of other musicians. |
| have influence on | ~에 영향력을 갖다 | Other musicians had a lot of influence on her. |
| affect (동사) | 직접적 영향을 미치다 | The worsening economy is affecting everybody. |
| effect (명사) | 영향, 효과 | TV has both positive and negative effects on children. |
| impact | 큰 영향 (직/간접 무관) | _(affect와 같은 방법으로 사용, 정도가 클 때)_ |

## 지금 막 시작했다 / 얼마 안됐다

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 이제 막 시작했어 | I just started + ing |
| 얼마 안됐어요 | It hasn't been very long. |
| 이제 막 익숙해지고 있어요 | I'm just getting used to it. |
| 아직 익숙해지고 있어요 | I'm still getting used to it. |
| ~에 익숙하다 | I'm used to + ing |

## 아까 / 이따가 / 얼마 전에 / 나중에

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 아까 (방금 전에) | earlier / earlier today |
| 얼마 전에 (이번 주/달) | earlier this week / this month |
| 이따가 | later today / later this evening |
| 나중에 | later this week / this month |

> `later`만 단독 사용은 권장하지 않음. 시간 표현과 함께 사용

**예시:**

* _I spoke with him on the phone earlier._ (아까 걔랑 통화했어)

## by vs until

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| until | 언제까지 지속적으로 | I'm going to study until 10 o'clock. |
| by | 기한/deadline | I have to finish this by 2 o'clock. |

> _I have to finish this until 2 o'clock._ (말이 안 됨)

## 알다 — 8가지 표현

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| know | 사실/정보를 알다 | I know his phone number. |
| understand | 이해하다, 알다 | I understand how you feel. |
| find out | 모르다가 알게 되다 | I found out about that just yesterday. |
| figure out | 방법/상황을 알아내다 | I figured out how to use it. |
| realize | 깨닫다 (머리 속에서만) | I finally realized what the problem was. |
| notice | 보고/듣고 알아차리다 | I noticed she had gotten a haircut. |
| recognize | 보고 뭔지/누군지 알아보다 | I didn't recognize him. |
| be aware of | 어떤 존재/상황을 인지하다 | I wasn't aware of that policy. |
| be familiar with | 잘 알다, 익숙한 | Sorry, I'm not very familiar with this topic. |
| tell (판단) | 판단이 안 되다 | I can't tell what it says. |

## 완전 잊고 있었다 (was supposed to)

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| was supposed to | 사전 합의하에 ~해야 했다/하기로 했었다 | I was supposed to be there by 8, but I arrived at 6. |
| not be supposed to | 하면 안 된다 (법/규정 등) | You're not supposed to be late on the first day. |

**예시:**

* _I totally forgot it was Sunday, for some reason, and I left way too early._
* _I was supposed to be there by 8 o'clock._

## spend

| 구조 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| spend on + 명사 | ~에 돈/시간을 쓰다 | I don't like to spend money on clothes. |
| waste on + 명사 | ~에 낭비하다 | We're wasting too much time on this project. |
| spend + ing | ~하면서 시간/돈을 쓰다 | We spent a lot of money preparing for the event. |

**예시:**

* _I'm going to spend Christmas at home watching TV._
* _Save up for ~_ — ~를 위해 저축하다

## prefer / I'd rather

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I prefer to V | ~를 더 좋아하다/선호하다 | I prefer to speak in English. |
| I'd prefer to V | (더 정중) ~하고 싶다 | I'd prefer to stay. |
| I'd rather V | 차라리 ~하겠다 (의견 강하게) | I'd rather die than feel that way again. |
| I'd rather A than B | B 하느니 차라리 A | I'd rather have a long commute than live in a small apartment. |
| I'd rather not V | 그렇게 하고 싶지 않다 | I'd rather not answer that right now. |

| --- | --- | --- |

## 하는 경우가 많다 / 대부분의 경우

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 거의 ~ 때문이야 | It's mostly because of ~ |
| 많은 경우에서 | A lot of the time / In many cases |
| 어떨 때는 | In some cases / Sometimes |
| 대부분의 경우 | In most cases / Most of the time |
| 보통 | Usually / Normally |

**예시:**

* _It's mostly because of my smartphone._
* _A lot of the time, I'm just web browsing._
* _In some cases, if I'm planning for a trip, I might be looking for information online._
* _In most cases, I just have a hard time staying away from my phone._

## 내가 하려는 말의 요점 정리

| 표현 | 뉘앙스 |
| --- | --- |
| My point is, ~ | 내 말은 (요점) |
| The point I'm trying to make is, ~ | 내 말은 (더 강조) |
| What I mean is, ~ | 내가 말하고자 하는 건 (의미 풀이) |
| What I'm trying to say is, ~ | (그러니까) 내가 말하고 싶은 건 |
| All I'm trying to say is, ~ | 내가 말하고자 한 건 이것뿐이야 (다른 의미 없음) |
| What's your point? | 그러니까 결론이 뭐야? _(친한 사이에만)_ |
| Get to the point | 요점만 말해 _(약간 공격적)_ |

**예시:**

* _My point is, there is no such thing as the perfect timing._
* _All I'm trying to say is, you should be careful. You've only known him for a couple of weeks._
* _I guess what I'm trying to say is, it's up to you._
* _It wouldn't hurt to try._ — 해봐서 나쁠 건 없다
* _There is no such thing as ~_ — 그런 ~같은 건 없다

## 영어 외국어 학습 관련 표현

* _You can't learn a foreign language with only books._
* _You have to familiarize yourself by constantly using it and practicing._
* _It's also important to experience the language through a variety of ways._
* _No matter what anybody says, there is no single perfect way to learn._
* _So you shouldn't rely on just a few methods._

**어휘:**

* _familiarize_ — 익숙하게 하다
* _constantly_ — 꾸준히, 계속
* _variety of ways_ — 여러 가지 방법
* _rely on_ — ~에 의존하다
