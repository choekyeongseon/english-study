## 📖 원문 아티클

Today's topic is aimed to get us to think about frozen food. (What do you know about frozen food?)

We are going to think of ideas around the topic.

**Article: Frozen food is Japan's 'dish of the year'**

Japanese food is famous all over the world. Everyone knows sushi, tempura and tofu. A lot of Japanese food is so well presented it looks like art.

It is therefore a little surprising that Japan's "dish of the year" for 2022 is frozen food. Each year, the Gurunavi Research Institute decides on the winner for its culinary award. The prize is given to food that highlights trends in what people eat. Frozen food got the prize in 2022 because of the Coronavirus pandemic.

Many people did not dine out at top restaurants. Instead, they stocked up on frozen food and ate at home. The winning frozen food was very basic - no examples of haute cuisine.

The judges selected a variety of frozen meat and pizzas – still in their air-tight bags.

Gurunavi said the pandemic has resulted in food companies experimenting with different ways of freezing food. They want to make sure that frozen food tastes good, even after it thaws out.

One of Japan's largest supermarkets has opened a specialized food shop called @Frozen. It sells over 1,500 different frozen food items. Japan's convenience stores have expanded their frozen food sections, and frozen food vending machines are popping up everywhere.

Some sell frozen sushi. Japan's Frozen Food Association said changing lifestyles have increased the popularity of frozen food.

Japan's population is aging, so many older people find frozen dishes more convenient than cooking from scratch.

심사위원들은 밀봉 포장된 다양한 냉동 고기와 피자를 선정했습니다.

구루나비는 팬데믹으로 인해 식품 회사들이 다양한 냉동 방법을 실험하게 되었다고 말했습니다. 그들은 냉동식품이 해동된 후에도 맛이 좋도록 하고 싶어 합니다.

일부는 냉동 초밥을 판매합니다. 일본냉동식품협회는 변화하는 생활양식이 냉동식품의 인기를 높였다고 말했습니다.

일본의 인구는 고령화되고 있어서, 많은 노인들이 직접 요리하는 것보다 냉동식품이 더 편리하다고 느낍니다.

## 📚 문법 포인트

### P1. `so + 형용사 + (that) + 결과절` — 결과를 나타내는 so ~ that

* 원문: `A lot of Japanese food is so well presented it looks like art.`
* 형태: `so + 형용사/부사 + (that) + 주어 + 동사`
* ✅ `The frozen pizza was so delicious (that) I bought three more.`
* 💡 `that`은 구어체에서 종종 생략됨

### P2. `It is + 형용사 + that ~` — 형식주어 구문

* 원문: `It is therefore a little surprising that Japan's "dish of the year" for 2022 is frozen food.`
* 형태: `It is + 형용사 + that + 주어 + 동사`
* ✅ `It is surprising that so many people prefer frozen food.`
* 💡 `that절`이 진주어, `It`은 형식주어

### P2. `is given to` — 수동태 (give의 수동)

* 원문: `The prize is given to food that highlights trends.`
* ✅ `The award is given to the best-selling product each year.`
* 💡 능동: `They give the prize to ~` → 수동: `The prize is given to ~`

### P3. `stock up on` — 사재기하다 / 비축하다

* 원문: `They stocked up on frozen food and ate at home.`
* ✅ `We stocked up on water and canned food before the typhoon.`
* 💡 `stock up on + 명사` = ~을 대량으로 사다 / 비축하다

### P5. `has resulted in + 동명사` — ~의 결과를 낳다

* 원문: `The pandemic has resulted in food companies experimenting with different ways.`
* 형태: `result in + 명사/동명사`
* ✅ `The new policy has resulted in many stores changing their hours.`
* 💡 `result in` = ~라는 결과를 낳다 (원인 → 결과)
* 비교: `result from` = ~에서 비롯되다 (결과 → 원인)

### P5. `make sure (that) + 주어 + 동사` — 확실히 하다

* 원문: `They want to make sure that frozen food tastes good.`
* ✅ `Make sure you check the expiration date before buying.`
* 💡 `make sure` 뒤에 `that절` 또는 `to부정사` 모두 가능

### P5. `even after + 주어 + 동사` — ~한 후에도

* 원문: `even after it thaws out`
* ✅ `The taste stays fresh even after you reheat it.`
* 💡 `even`이 강조의 역할: "~한 후에조차도"

### P6. `pop up` — (갑자기) 나타나다 / 등장하다

* 원문: `Frozen food vending machines are popping up everywhere.`
* 형태: `be + popping up` (현재진행)
* ✅ `New coffee shops are popping up all over the city.`
* 💡 예상치 못한 곳에 갑자기 나타나는 느낌

### P7. `have increased` — 현재완료 (변화의 결과)

* 원문: `Changing lifestyles have increased the popularity of frozen food.`
* ✅ `Online shopping has increased dramatically since the pandemic.`
* 💡 과거에 시작된 변화가 현재까지 영향을 미칠 때 현재완료 사용

### P8. `find + 목적어 + 형용사` — ~이 ~하다고 느끼다

* 원문: `Many older people find frozen dishes more convenient than cooking from scratch.`
* 형태: `find + 목적어 + 형용사(보어)`
* ✅ `I find frozen pizza quite tasty these days.`
* 💡 `think`보다 경험에 기반한 판단의 뉘앙스

### P8. `from scratch` — 처음부터 / 밑바닥부터

* ✅ `I made this pasta sauce from scratch — no store-bought ingredients.`
* 💡 요리뿐만 아니라 다양한 상황에서 사용: `build a website from scratch`

**나의 초안:**

> I'm big fan of a frozen food. I usually go to shopping onece a week and bought some frozen food such frozen dumpling or donkazu. 퇴근 후 빠르게 먹을수 있다는 점이 좋다. 그래서 나는 이번 주제는 낯설지 않았다.

**핵심 표현:**

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| ~의 열렬한 팬이다 | a big fan of ~ |

| ~같은 (예시 나열) | such as ~ |

* `I'm big fan` → `I'm a big fan` (관사 a 필수)

* `such frozen dumpling` → `such as frozen dumplings` (such as + 복수)

* `recently` → `these days` (현재 지속 상황은 these days)
* `too much` + 불가산명사 / `too many` + 가산명사
* "내 입맛에 맞게" → `to my liking` / `the way I like it`

### Q7. What do you think of frozen pizza?

**나의 초안:**

> 나는 냉동피자를 좋아하지 않는다. 한달전에 산 피자가 아직 냉동실에 있다. 맛이 없는것 같다. 그리고 난 피자를 좋아하지 않는다

**나의 최종 문장:**

> "I don't like frozen pizza. I bought one about a month ago, and it's still in my freezer. I don't think it tastes good. Actually, I don't really like pizza in general."

**핵심 표현:**

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 한 달 전에 하나 샀다 | I bought one about a month ago |
| 아직 냉동실에 있다 | it's still in my freezer |
| 맛있다고 생각하지 않는다 | I don't think it tastes good |
| 사실 피자를 전반적으로 좋아하지 않는다 | I don't really like pizza in general |

**교정 포인트:**

* `freezer`(냉동실) vs `fridge`(냉장고) 구분
* "맛없는 것 같다" → `I don't think it tastes good` (부정 전이: 부정을 앞으로)
* `in general` = 전반적으로
* `Actually`로 시작하면 본심을 드러내는 뉘앙스

### Q8. Do you like to cook from scratch?

**나의 초안:**

> 나는 요리를 잘하지 못한다. 칼질도 잘 못한다. 하지만 와이프와 둘이서 음식을 만드는 과정이 재미있다. 맛은 때때로 없지만 그래도 즐겁다

**나의 최종 문장:**

> "I'm not a good cook. I can't even chop ingredients well. But I find the process of cooking with my wife really fun. The food doesn't always taste great, but we still enjoy it."

**핵심 표현:**

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 요리를 잘 못한다 | I'm not a good cook |
| 칼질조차도 잘 못한다 | I can't even chop ingredients well |
| ~하는 과정이 재미있다고 느낀다 | I find the process of ~ really fun |
| 항상 맛있진 않다 | doesn't always taste great |
| 그래도 즐겁다 | we still enjoy it |

**교정 포인트:**

* "요리를 못한다" → `I'm not a good cook` (회화체로 자연스러움)
* `chop`(토막), `slice`(얇게), `dice`(깍둑), `mince`(잘게 다지다)
* `find + 목적어 + 형용사` 패턴 활용!
* "때때로 맛없다" → `doesn't always taste great` (부드러운 표현)
* `wife` → `my wife` (영어에서는 소유격 필수)
