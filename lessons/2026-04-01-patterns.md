## 📌 핵심 표현 정리

### 1. 기대와 예상 (Expectation)

| 표현 | 의미 | 포인트 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **look forward to + -ing** | 기대하다 (들뜬 마음) | 뒤에 **동사원형이 아닌 -ing**가 오는 것이 핵심! |
| **I was looking forward to...** | 기대했는데 못 해서 아쉬움 | 과거형으로 쓰면 아쉬운 뉘앙스 |
| **can't wait to + V** | 너무 기대된다 (빨리 하고 싶다) | `look forward to`보다 더 강하고 캐주얼한 설렘 |
| **expect + 사람 + to V** | 예상하다 (그럴 것이라 믿음) | "당연히 ~하겠지"라는 기대를 담을 때 |

### 2. 영향과 효과 (Influence & Impact)

| 표현 | 의미 | 포인트 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **affect** (동사) | 직접적 영향을 미치다 | 주어에 따라 `affects`처럼 수 일치 주의 |
| **have an influence on** | ~에 영향력을 갖다 | 간접적이거나 지속적인 영향 |
| **impact** (명사/동사) | 큰 영향 (강한 느낌) | `affect`보다 결과나 파급력이 더 클 때 |

### 3. ~하기로 되어 있다 (Be supposed to)

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **be supposed to + V** | (계획/예보/규칙상) ~하기로 되어 있다 | It's supposed to rain this weekend. |
| **was supposed to + V** | ~하기로 되어 있었다 (못 했을 때) | I was supposed to go to the library today. |

💡 `be supposed to` vs `will` 차이:

| 표현 | 뉘앙스 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| It **will** rain. | 단정적 확신 — "비가 올 거야" | 화자가 확신할 때 |
| It's **supposed to** rain. | 외부 정보 근거 — "비 온대요" | 일기예보 등 외부 정보에 기반 (원어민이 일상에서 훨씬 자주 사용!) |
| The forecast says it's **going to** rain. | 예보 인용 — "예보에서 비 온다고 하네요" | 출처를 명시할 때 |

## 📚 문법 포인트

### `look forward to + -ing` — to 뒤에 동명사!

* `to`가 전치사이기 때문에 뒤에 -ing가 옴 (to부정사가 아님!)
* ✅ `I'm looking forward to meeting you.`
* ❌ `I'm looking forward to meet you.`
* 💡 과거형으로 쓰면 아쉬움: `I was looking forward to it, but it got cancelled.`

### `affect` vs `effect` — 품사 구분

* `affect` = **동사** (영향을 미치다): `The weather affects my plans.`
* `effect` = **명사** (영향/결과): `The weather has an effect on my plans.`
* 💡 헷갈릴 때: **A**ffect = **A**ction(동사), **E**ffect = **E**nd result(명사)

### `have an influence on` — 전치사 주의

* ✅ `Coffee has an influence on my concentration.`
* ❌ `I have an influence on coffee.` (내가 커피에 영향을 미친다는 뜻이 돼버림!)
* 💡 **주어**가 영향을 주는 쪽, **on 뒤**가 영향을 받는 쪽

### `expect + 목적어 + to V` — 예상하다

* ✅ `I didn't expect it to rain yesterday.`
* ❌ `I didn't expected it was ranning.` (시제/스펠링 주의)
* 💡 `didn't` 뒤에는 항상 **동사원형**: `didn't expect` (❌ `didn't expected`)

### `be supposed to` — 의문문 어순

* 평서문: `We are supposed to meet at 3.`
* ✅ 의문문: `What time **are we** supposed to meet?`
* ❌ 의문문: `What time **we are** supposed to meet?`
* 💡 의문사 + be동사 + 주어 순서!

## 💬 회화 연습

### Q1. 날씨와 주말 계획

**나의 초안:**

> I was actually thinking of going to hiking this weekend but I heard it will be going to rain this weekend. I was looking forward to the activities but now I'm 실망스러운 about the 소식. So the weather affect my plan.

**교정 문장 (Corrected):**

> I was thinking of going hiking, but I heard it will rain this weekend. I was looking forward to it, but now I'm disappointed about the news. The weather affects my plans.

**더 자연스러운 문장 (Natural):**

> I was planning to go hiking this weekend, but it's supposed to rain. I was really looking forward to it, so I'm pretty bummed out by the news. The weather always has an impact on my plans.

**교정 포인트:**

| 틀린 표현 | 올바른 표현 | 설명 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| going to hiking | going hiking | go + -ing (go hiking, go shopping, go swimming) |
| it will be going to rain | it's supposed to rain | will과 be going to 중복 사용 금지 / supposed to가 더 자연스러움 |
| 실망스러운 | disappointed | "실망한" 감정을 나타내는 형용사 |
| 소식 | the news | news는 항상 단수 취급 (a news ❌) |
| the weather affect | the weather affects | 3인칭 단수 주어 → affects (수 일치) |
| my plan | my plans | 여러 계획이므로 복수 |

**💡 추가 표현:**

* `bummed out` = 실망한/기분 다운된 (캐주얼)
* `has an impact on` = ~에 영향을 미치다

### Q2. 도서관 근처 생활

**나의 초안:**

> Yes! I live near the Hwaseong Library, but unfortunally I don't have time to spend in the library because i'm recently quite busy of my work.

**교정 문장 (Corrected):**

> Yes! I live near the Hwaseong Library, but unfortunately I don't have time to spend there because I've been quite busy with my work recently.

**교정 포인트:**

| 틀린 표현 | 올바른 표현 | 설명 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| unfortunally | unfortunately | 스펠링 주의! |
| i'm | I'm | I는 항상 대문자 |
| busy of | busy with | busy + with (전치사 조합 고정) |
| i'm recently quite busy | I've been quite busy recently | "최근 계속 바쁜 상태"는 현재완료 진행이 자연스러움 |

> ✅ I'm looking forward to having a nice dinner with my wife this weekend.

---

**연습 2: "커피는 나의 집중력에 큰 영향을 미쳐"**

**나의 초안:** I have an influence on coffee at work.

> ❌ 이렇게 쓰면 "내가 커피에 영향을 미친다"는 뜻!

> ✅ Coffee has a big influence on my concentration at work.  
> ✅ Coffee affects my focus at work.

💡 **주어-목적어 방향 주의!** 영향을 주는 것이 주어, 받는 것이 on 뒤에!

---

**연습 3: "어제 비가 올 것이라고 예상하지 못했어"**

**나의 초안:** I didn't expected it was ranning yesterday.

> ✅ I didn't expect it to rain yesterday.

| 틀린 표현 | 올바른 표현 | 설명 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| didn't expected | didn't expect | didn't 뒤는 항상 동사원형 |
| it was ranning | it to rain | expect + 목적어 + to V 패턴 |
| ranning | raining | 스펠링: rain → raining (n 하나!) |
