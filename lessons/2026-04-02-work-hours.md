## 📄 전체 본문

### Article

**A four-day work week in Japan increased productivity**

Today's topic is aimed to get us to think about work hours.  
We are going to think of ideas around the topic (What do you know about productivity?)

The technology company Microsoft has said it successfully trialed a four-day working week in Japan, which saw improvements in workers' productivity and job satisfaction.

The trial took place over the summer at the tech giant's headquarters in Tokyo. Employees were given every Friday off as paid leave in August. The company monitored the effects a three-day weekend would have on workers' performance.

Company executives said the shorter working week resulted in additional benefits. Productivity rose by 40 per cent and 92 per cent of employees expressed their satisfaction with the scheme.

The trial could lead to a change in Japan's infamous workplace practices of long hours and lots of overtime.

Microsoft called the trial the Work-Life Choice Challenge. The company said it aimed, "to create an environment where each employee can choose a diverse and flexible way of working according to the circumstances of their work and life".

Other aspects of the trial included shortening the length of meetings to a maximum of 30 minutes and encouraging workers to choose online chats over face-to-face ones.

There were additional advantages to the trial. Microsoft said that 23 per cent less electricity was consumed and 59 per cent fewer pages were printed compared with August 2018.

Microsoft will conduct another experiment in Japan later this year and has asked employees for their input.

### 문단 3

> The trial took place over the summer at the tech giant's headquarters in Tokyo. Employees were given every Friday off as paid leave in August. The company monitored the effects a three-day weekend would have on workers' performance.

**번역:** 이 시험 운영은 도쿄에 있는 이 기술 대기업의 본사에서 여름 동안 진행되었습니다. 직원들은 8월 한 달간 매주 금요일을 유급 휴가로 받았습니다. 회사는 3일간의 주말이 직원들의 업무 성과에 미치는 영향을 모니터링했습니다.

| 단어/표현 | 의미 |
| --- | --- |
| take place | 일어나다, 개최되다 |
| tech giant | 기술 대기업 |
| headquarters | 본사 |
| paid leave | 유급 휴가 |
| monitor | 모니터링하다 |
| performance | 성과, 실적 |
| three-day weekend | 금+토+일 = 3일 연휴 |

**문법 포인트:**

* 수동태 `were given` = 직원들이 금요일을 받았다 (회사가 준 것을 직원 입장에서 표현)

**심화 문법: the effects (that) a three-day weekend would have**

```
The company monitored the effects [a three-day weekend would have on workers' performance].
```

### 문단 4

> Company executives said the shorter working week resulted in additional benefits. Productivity rose by 40 per cent and 92 per cent of employees expressed their satisfaction with the scheme.

**번역:** 회사 임원들은 더 짧은 근무 주가 추가적인 이점을 가져왔다고 말했습니다. 생산성은 40% 상승했고, 직원의 92%가 이 제도에 대한 만족감을 표현했습니다.

| 단어/표현 | 의미 |
| --- | --- |
| executives | 임원, 경영진 |
| result in | ~을 초래하다 |
| result from | ~로부터 비롯되다 |
| additional | 추가적인 |
| rose | 상승했다 (rise의 과거형) |
| scheme /skiːm/ | 제도, 계획 (발음: 스킴) |

**문법 포인트:**

* `result in` vs `result from`

    * result in = ~을 초래하다 (원인→결과)
    * result from = ~로부터 비롯되다 (결과←원인)
    
* `rise` vs `raise`

    * rise (자동사): Productivity rose. (생산성이 올랐다)
    * raise (타동사): They raised productivity. (그들이 올렸다)

### 문단 6

### 문단 7

> Other aspects of the trial included shortening the length of meetings to a maximum of 30 minutes and encouraging workers to choose online chats over face-to-face ones.

**번역:** 시험 운영의 다른 측면으로는 회의 시간을 최대 30분으로 단축하고, 직원들이 대면 대화보다 온라인 채팅을 선택하도록 장려하는 것이 포함되었습니다.

| 단어/표현 | 의미 |
| --- | --- |
| aspects | 측면 |
| maximum | 최대 |
| face-to-face | 대면의 |
| ones | 앞에 나온 명사(chats)를 대신하는 대명사 → 한국어의 "것/거" |

**문법 포인트:**

* `include + 동명사(-ing)`: included shortening / encouraging
* `A over B`: B보다 A를 선택하다 (choose online chats **over** face-to-face ones)
* `ones` = 앞 명사 반복을 피하는 대명사 (= 한국어의 "것")

    * `the big thing` vs `the big one`
    
        * the big thing = 새로운 개념 강조 ("핵심은 ~이야")
        * the big one = 앞서 언급한 것들 중 큰 것을 지칭

### 문단 8

> There were additional advantages to the trial. Microsoft said that 23 per cent less electricity was consumed and 59 per cent fewer pages were printed compared with August 2018.

**번역:** 시험 운영에는 추가적인 이점들이 있었습니다. 마이크로소프트는 2018년 8월과 비교했을 때 전기 소비량이 23% 감소했고, 인쇄 페이지 수가 59% 줄었다고 밝혔습니다.

**문법 포인트:**

* 수동태 강조: 누가 소비했는지보다 **결과**가 중요할 때 수동태 사용
* `less` vs `fewer`

|  | less | fewer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 사용 | 셀 수 없는 명사 | 셀 수 있는 명사 |
| 예시 | less electricity | fewer pages |

> ⚠️ "less people"은 문법적으로 틀리지만 원어민도 일상에서 자주 사용. 공식적인 상황에서는 "fewer people" 사용!

* `compared with` vs `compared to`

    * compared with = 차이점 분석 (수치 비교)
    * compared to = 유사점 중심 비교

## 🌏 문화 비교: 한국 vs 일본 과로 문화

### 🇯🇵 일본 - Karoshi (過労死)

> Japan has a concept called "Karoshi" which literally means "death from overwork." It became an official cause of death in Japan in the 1980s. Japanese workers often feel pressured to stay late at the office, even if they have finished their work, out of respect for their superiors.

### 🇰🇷 한국 - Gwarosa & Ppalli-ppalli (빨리빨리)

> Korea has a similar concept. Koreans are famous for their "Ppalli-ppalli" culture, which means "hurry hurry." This creates a high-pressure work environment. Korea actually had one of the longest working hours among OECD countries. In 2018, Korea introduced a 52-hour workweek law to address this issue.

### 📊 연간 평균 근무시간 비교 (OECD)

| 국가 | 연간 평균 근무시간 |
| --- | --- |
| 🇰🇷 한국 | 약 1,901시간 |
| 🇯🇵 일본 | 약 1,607시간 |
| 🇩🇪 독일 | 약 1,341시간 |
| 🌍 OECD 평균 | 약 1,716시간 |

### 💬 수업에서 활용할 표현

> "This article reminds me of Korea's work culture too. Like Japan, Korea also has a culture of long working hours. We even have a similar word — 'Gwarosa.' But in 2018, the Korean government introduced a 52-hour workweek to improve work-life balance."
