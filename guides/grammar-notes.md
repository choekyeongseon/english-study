# 문법 (Grammar Notes)

핵심 문법 항목별 정리

---

## must be / must have been (강한 추측)

| 시제 | 구조 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 현재 추측 | must be | You must be really hungry. |
| 과거 추측 | must have been | You must've been really hungry. |

**예시 패턴**

* _I haven't eaten anything all day._ → _You must be really hungry._
* _I didn't eat anything all day yesterday._ → _You must've been really hungry._
* _That must be really tough._ (현재 반복 상황이 힘들겠다)
* _That must've been really tough._ (한 번의 일로 힘들었겠다)

> 조동사(must/should..)와 현재완료가 결합하면 현재가 아닌 과거 시점에 대한 이야기가 됨

---

## should — 의무/충고/예상

| 용법 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 의무/충고 | ~하는 게 좋겠다 (must보다 약함) | You should obey your parents. |
| 예상/기대 | 아마 ~일 것이다 (= will probably) | They should be here soon. |
| 명사절 사용 | advise/suggest/insist 뒤 | He advised that I should take a rest. |

---

## used to — 과거의 습관/상태

| 구조 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| used to + 동사원형 | 과거 습관 (~하곤 했다) | We used to go swimming. |
| used to + be | 과거 상태였다 | There used to be a hut on this hill. |
| be used to + ing | ~하는 데 익숙하다 | She is used to studying at night. |
| be used to + 동사원형 | ~하는데 사용되다 | This flour is used to bake apple pies. |
| get used to + 명사 | ~에 익숙해지다 | They got used to eating Korean food. |

## be / get / become

| 동사 | 의미 | 용법 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| be | 상태 (~이다) | We were complete strangers. |
| get | 상태의 변화 (~되다) | We got really close. |
| become | 변화의 결과 (~가 됐다) | We have become best friends. |

**예시 흐름:**

1. _1년 전까지만 해도 우리는 완전 모르는 사이였어._ → **were** complete strangers
2. _어떤 파티에서 만나고 나서, 우리는 완전 가까워졌어._ → **got** really close
3. _지금은 제일 친한 친구가 됐어._ → **have become** best friends

## bored vs boring (감정 형용사)

| 형태 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| boring | 지루하게 만드는 (원인) | The movie was boring. |
| bored | 지루해진 (결과, 감정) | I'm bored. |
| confusing | 헷갈리게 만드는 | It's confusing. |
| confused | 헷갈리는 (감정) | I'm confused. |
| exciting | 흥미롭게 만드는 | It's exciting. |
| excited | 흥분된, 기대되는 (감정) | I'm so excited about the trip. |
| interesting | 흥미롭게 만드는 | That's very interesting. |
| interested in | ~에 관심이 있는 | I'm interested in this. |

> `fun` — 오락/게임이 재미있을 때
> `interesting` — 호기심을 자극하거나 신기할 때

## 간접화법 (Indirect Speech)

**현재 → 과거 시제 변환 규칙**

| 직접화법 | 간접화법 |
| --- | --- |
| "I am ~" | she said she **was** ~ |
| "I will ~" | she said she **would** ~ |
| "Can I ~?" | she asked if she **could** ~ |

**예시:**

* _She said she was on her way to lunch, and asked me if I wanted to join her._
* _I told her I was in the middle of something, so I asked her if she could wait._
* _And she said, she would be downstairs._
* _I told her, I would be there as soon as I can._
* _She said, I didn't need to hurry._

**핵심 패턴**

* **on the way to ~** — ~가는 길
* **asked ~, if ~** — ~한테 ~하자고 물어봤어
* **will → would** — 간접화법에서 미래 → 과거

## I like + to / I like + ing 차이

| 구조 | 강조 포인트 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I like + to V | 행동의 결과/효과 | I like to get up early. (그래서 자주 그렇게 한다) |
| I like + ing | 행동의 순간/경험 자체 | I like getting up early. (그 자체가 좋아) |

**예시:**

* _I like to work overtime._ → 돈을 더 받을 수 있어서
* _I like working overtime._ → 일하면서 배울 수 있어서
* _When I take notes, I like writing with a pencil._ → 쓸 때 나는 소리가 좋아
* _I like to write with a pencil._ → 정정이 쉬워서

## do / be 질문형

| 종류 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| do you ~? | 평소에/일반적으로 하는지 | Do you drive to work? |
| did you ~? | 과거에 했는지 | What did you do yesterday? |
| are you going to ~? | 앞으로 할 건지 | What are you going to do today? |
| is it ~? | 현재 상태 | Is it good? |
| was it ~? | 과거 상태 | Was it good? |
| will it ~? | 미래 판단/인상 | It will be fun. |

> `going to`는 앞으로 정해진 느낌이 강해서, 일반적인 인상(재밌을 거야, 어려울 거야)에는 `will`이 더 자연스럽다

## as ~ as 비교 구문

### Type 1 — 특징/성격/성질 비교

**구조:** be동사 + as + 형용사/부사 + as + 비교 대상

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 내 친구는 나만큼 키가 크지 않아요 | My friend is not as tall as me. |
| 오늘이 어제보다 춥지 않은 것 같아 | I think today is not as cold as yesterday. |
| 원작이 리메이크보다 안 좋은 것 같다 | I don't think the original is as good as the remake. |

### Type 2 — 정도/빈도 비교

**구조:** 액션동사 + (목적어) + as + 형용사/부사 + as + 비교 대상

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 저는 다른 사람들만큼 빨리 못 먹어요 | I can't eat as quickly as others. |
| 저는 그녀만큼 자주 운동하지 않아요 | I don't work out as often as her. |
| 저는 부모님만큼 일찍 안 일어나요 | I don't get up as early as my parents. |

> 자동사: `as often as her` (○) / `as often as she does` (×, 목적어 없음)
> 타동사: `as much as I do` (○) / `as much as me` (×, 의미 달라짐)

### Type 3 — 양(量) 비교

**구조:** 동사 + as + much/many + 명사 + as + 비교 대상

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 나는 너만큼 시간이 많지 않아 | I don't have as much time as you. |
| 걔는 우리만큼 돈을 많이 못 벌어 | He can't make as much money as we do. |
| 나는 너만큼 거기에 많이 안 가봤어 | I haven't been there as many times as you. |

### Type 4 — 다른 시점/상황과의 비교

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 학생이었을 때만큼 성격이 외향적이지 않았어요 | When I was a student, I wasn't as outgoing as I am now. |
| 내 핸드폰이 처음 샀을 때만큼 빠르지 않아요 | My phone is not as fast as it was when I first got it. |
| 20년 전에는 지금만큼 취직하는 게 어렵지 않았어 | About 20 years ago, getting a job was not as hard as it is now. |
| 걔는 처음 만났을 때만큼 그녀에게 잘 해주지 않아 | He doesn't treat her as well as he used to when they first met. |

**비교 표현 다른 방식:**

* _I like pizza, but not as much as I used to._
* _A: Do you enjoy drinking? B: Not as much as I used to._

## which — 덧붙이는 관계절

문장 뒤에 `which`를 써서 코멘트나 부가 설명을 붙이는 패턴

| 두 문장 | which 결합 |
| --- | --- |
| I got up at 7. It was earlier than usual. | I got up at 7, **which** was earlier than usual. |
| I had breakfast. I don't do that very often. | I had breakfast, **which** I don't do very often. |
| There was a meeting. It lasted more than 2 hours. | There was a meeting **which** lasted more than 2 hours. |

> `which`를 해석할 때: "그리고 그것은/을/를..."

## if vs whether

| 표현 | 용법 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| if | 여러 가능성 중 하나인지 (or not) | I don't know if he's coming. |
| whether | ~이든 아니든 | I don't know whether he's coming (or not). |
| if | 조건 | I'll buy it if you want. |
| whether | 어느 쪽이든 | I'll buy it whether you want or not. |

## get — 4가지 용법

### 1. 움직임만을 나타낼 때

* _Get up_ — 위로 움직이다
* _Get over_ — 너머로 움직이다, 극복하다
* _Get out_ — 밖으로 나가다

### 2. 획득하다

* _I got a job._ — 일자리를 얻었다
* _He got a speeding ticket._ — 과속 딱지를 받았다

### 3. ~되다, ~되어지다 (get + 형용사)

* _Are you angry?_ → 현재 화난 상태
* _Did you get angry?_ → 화가 난 상태로 변화
* _Let's go before it gets cold._ — 추워지기 전에

### 4. ~하게끔 하다 (get + 목적어 + pp)

* _Can you get this done today?_
* _I got my car repaired._

## being — 동명사 용법

| 용법 | 예문 |
| --- | --- |
| being + 형용사 (명사처럼) | Being alone is scary. |
| is/are being + 형용사 (일시적 상태) | She is being nice. (지금 잠시 친절하게 구는 중) |
| is + 형용사 (항상 그런 상태) | She is nice. (성격이 친절하다) |

**예시:**

* _He is being rude._ — 지금 무례하게 구는 중
* _Why are you being so mean to me?_
* _Stop being harsh on yourself._

## 단순현재 vs 현재진행

| 시제 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 단순현재 | 늘/매일/규칙적으로 | I work out. (항상 운동하는 사람) |
| 현재진행 | 지금 이 순간/잠시 | I'm working out. (지금 하고 있는 중) |

## 감각동사 패턴 (look, sound, smell, taste, feel)

### Pattern 1 — 감각동사 + 형용사

* _It looked really serious._ — 되게 심각해보였어
* _It feels really heavy._ — 정말 무거운 느낌이야
* _It was really awkward. → It felt really awkward._ (감각을 통해 느낀 것)
* _It looked really dirty._ — 정말 더러워 보였어

### Pattern 2 — 감각동사 + like + 명사

* _This looks like real leather._ — 진짜 가죽 같아보여
* _This looks weird, it kind of looks like a cigar._

### Pattern 3 — 감각동사 + like + 완성된 문장

* _It sounded like it was a really serious problem._
* _It feels like I walked all day._
* _It looks like it's going to rain._
* _It looked like it hadn't been washed in months._ — 몇 달간 씻지 않은 것처럼 보였어

### Pattern 4 — 확신/비유 표현과 결합

* _It was pretty clear that it didn't have an owner._ — 보나마나 주인이 없는 거였어
* _It almost looked as if it had nothing left but skin and bones._ — 마치 가죽과 뼈밖에 안 남은 것처럼 보였어

> `be` 동사와의 차이: 감각을 통해 느낀 것 vs 사실을 말하는 것
> _This looks really expensive._ vs _This is really expensive._

> **관계절 생략**: 묘사 문장에서 흔히 생략 가능
> _I saw a man who was smoking → I saw a man smoking_

## 비례식 표현

**구조:** the + 비교급 ~, the + 비교급 ~

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 많을수록 좋아 | The more, the better. |
| 이를수록 좋아 | The earlier, the better. |
| 가까울수록 좋아 | The closer, the better. |
| 작을수록 가격이 더 싸 | The smaller it is, the cheaper it is. |
| 기능이 많을수록 더 편해 | The more functions it has, the more convenient it is. |

## 완료시제 (have + p.p.) — 4가지 용법

| 용법 | 의미 | 표현 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 경험 | ~한 적이 있다 | ever | Have you ever been to New York? |
| 계속 | ~해왔다 | since | I've lived here since 2010. |
| 완료 | ~했다 (방금) | just, already, yet | I've already seen the movie. |
| 결과 | ~해버렸다 (현재까지 영향) | — | She has left. (지금 없다) |

**Do you ever ~ ?**

* "~한 적 있니?", "평소에 ~하곤 하니?"
* _Do you ever dream you are flying?_
* _Do you ever get cravings for donuts?_

## 조동사 완료시제 (추측)

| 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| must have + p.p. | ~했나봐 (강한 추측) | She must've had some problems with her boss. |
| might have + p.p. | ~했을 수도 있다 (가능성) | She might have had some health problems. |
| couldn't have + p.p. | ~했을 리가 없다 (부정) | It couldn't have been because she was sick of the job. |
| could have + p.p. | ~할 수 있었을 텐데 | It could have been a little embarrassing. |
| would have + p.p. | ~했을 텐데 (반사실) | If I had gone to bed earlier, I wouldn't have been so tired. |

## 가정법 — 이럴 줄 알았으면

**구조:** If I had known ~, I would have / wouldn't have + p.p.

| 한국어 | 영어 |
| --- | --- |
| 그게 지루한 줄 알았더라면, 시작 안 했을 텐데 | If I had known it's boring, I wouldn't have started it. |
| 걔 상황을 알았더라면, 돈 얘기는 안 했을 텐데 | If I had known about his situation, I wouldn't have talked about money. |
| 먼저 전화해서 확인했더라면, 시간을 낭비 안 했을 텐데 | If I had called and checked first, I wouldn't have wasted so much time. |
